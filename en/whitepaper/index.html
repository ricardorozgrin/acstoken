THE GUARDIAN OF THE AMAZON FOREST IN THE STATE OF ACRE/BRAZIL
Version 1.0
May, 2022

The Acre Carbon Standard Token (ACS Token) is a token developed by Companhia de Desenvolvimento e Serviços Ambientais (CDSA) of the state of Acre in Brazil. It is a smart contract registered on the Ethereum blockchain (ERC20 standard) that guarantees full transparency, traceability and the effective neutralization of greenhouse gas emissions.

It is a digital asset based on carbon credits from carbon sequestration in conservation and forest preservation projects in the Amazon in a jurisdictional context (J-REDD+) that is registered with IHS Markit, one of the largest certifiers of carbon credits. in the world - based in Germany.

Each unit of the ACS Token is equivalent to one ton of carbon dioxide (1 tCO2e), which was sequestered through government programs to reduce deforestation and conserve forest areas in the state of Acre (SISA Law).

" Our mission is to make easy the connection between the credits generated by the Amazon forest in the state of Acre and the people, companies or governments that want to offset their emissions. ACS Token was created to be the technological platform that will guarantee full traceability and reliability in this process". José Gondim Santos - President of CDSA

What is SISA

In recent decades, the State of Acre has developed efforts to implement a jurisdictional system for emission reductions, based on a robust legal framework with the Law of the State System of Incentives to Environmental Services - SISA, Law 2.308/2010, which establishes a system of promotion and encouragement of environmental services, with a regulatory governance structure, supervision, monitoring, reporting and verification added to the registration and transparency of environmental assets.

In this context, a reference "standard" called ACS - Acre Carbon Standard was created, which served over the years as a foundation for raising financial resources and effective payment for emission reductions with accredited international institutions such as KFW - German Development Bank - with its “Early Movers” REDD Program, which has already accounted for more than 20 million tons removed from the market, having the referred standard been created by the Scientific Committee composed of scientists and renowned internationally recognized professionals, being registered with IHS Markit, in the link: https://mer.markit.com/br-reg/public/project.jsp?project_id=103000000005599

What is CDSA/SA

CDSA/SA – Companhia de Serviços Ambientais – is responsible for promoting sustainable development and linking itself to voluntary regional and sectoral systems, which allow promoting and increasing the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions and encouraging a low carbon economy. , exploring opportunities related to economic development, social well-being and ecosystem balance, as well as managing and disposing, to the extent of its competence, the assets and credits resulting from ecosystem services and products arising from programs, subprograms, plans and projects, all pursuant to art. 15, items I, II, III, IV, V, VI, VII and VIII of Law 2,308/10.

CDSA/SA has an institutional and legal governance structure, designed to seek strength and comfort for donors, financiers and investors in the context of promoting and investing in actions aimed at reducing emissions and developing a sustainable and low-carbon economy, in order to comply with the provisions of art. 39, items I, II, III and §1 and 2 of State Law n. 2,308/2010.

What are carbon credits?

The Carbon Credit, or Certified Emissions Reduction (CER) is a certificate issued by an individual, legal or governmental person, which reduced its emission of greenhouse gases (GHG), the development of this mechanism was born in 1997, from the Kyoto protocol.

One of the mechanisms is the adoption of a Clean Development Mechanism (CDM). Another mechanism is REDD (Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and Forest Degradation; in Portuguese, "Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and Forest Degradation") was a mechanism to encourage forest conservation, reducing degradation and deforestation. As of COP-26, the creation of the J-REED (jurisdictional REDD+) was debated, that is, projects that are under the jurisdiction of government entities.

In the case of the ACS Token, our carbon credits are credits arising from the program of the State System of Incentives to Environmental Services - SISA, Law 2,308/2010, focused on reducing deforestation in the state of Acre, thus being a J-REDD+ program. This program was financed by KFW – German Development Bank – with its REDD Program “Early Movers”, and already accounts for more than 20 million tons of carbon dioxide offset.

The Carbon Credit is a unit of measurement that has a ratio of 1 credit equals 1 ton of CO2 removed in a given year. All credits are audited by international institutions, registered on the platforms of these organizations following strict protocols. The ACS Token adds a layer of traceability through Blockchain technology that records the transfers of credits, as well as ensures that there is no double counting of the retirement (compensation) of the ACS Token happens only once (by using the “Burn” function). of the Smart Contract).

About the market

The carbon market emerged from ECO-92, held in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, within the framework of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC). Brazil was the first country to adhere to this treaty, which established that the 150 signatory countries should limit or reduce their emissions of polluting gases.

Thus, reducing emissions started to generate economic value for countries. It was in this convention that it was established that a ton of carbon dioxide (CO2) corresponds to a carbon credit, which can be traded on the international market. Subsequently, in 2007, the Kyoto Protocol consolidated the rules and methodologies and in the Paris Agreement, in 2015, the carbon market gained more strength, as the signatory nations decided to act together to combat global warming, with the establishment of ambitious targets. . Currently, 194 countries have signed the agreements. In Brazil, it is estimated that the value of the carbon market will be between 13 to 48 billion dollars by 2030, especially in REED projects.

The creation of carbon markets aims to allow people or organizations to neutralize or offset their greenhouse gas emissions by purchasing carbon credits, generated by GHG sequestration projects, whether under Clean Development Mechanisms (CDM) or REED+ .

Currently, one of the challenges in this market was to make the connection between the credit generators, the verification and control entities and a person or small company that wanted to offset a small volume of emissions. The ACS Token was created exactly to be this technological platform that will guarantee traceability and reliability in this process.

Climate changes

All human activities have some impact on the environment. However, from the 18th century onwards, the Industrial Revolution that took place based on fossil fuels (coal and oil), GHG emissions increased exponentially, to the point that at the end of the 20th century, it was scientifically verified that climate patterns were being modified. as a result of the accumulation of these GHGs in the atmosphere. There were also other impacts such as the increase in industrial activities, unsustainable land use, dumping of pollutants into rivers, lakes and seas, deforestation, among other impacts.

In addition to these impacts, the entire structure of human society will be heavily impacted by climate change, such as:

The rise in sea level due to the melting of the poles, causing the inundation of coastal cities and the subsequent mass migration of these populations; The desertification of arable areas with the reduction of food supply; Extreme weather events such as hurricanes, storms, or prolonged droughts; Among countless others. Therefore, the democratization of access to compensation mechanisms and the establishment of a robust payment structure for environmental services has become an urgent action for all.

Therefore, ACS Token was born with this purpose: Democratizing access to compensation mechanisms and enabling a robust payment program for environmental services for residents of the State of Acre.

Important Terms and Concepts

Smart Contract: Technological solution in blockchain on the Ethereum platform, where all ACS Tokens are created, and which records all transactions, transfers and retirements through unique identifier codes.

ACS Token: is the digital unit for a Carbon Credit generated and registered in the context of the SISA project. The amount of the ACS Token is limited to the maximum total of credits generated and registered by CDSA/SA on IHS Markit.

Mint: is the action of adding new ACS Tokens to the Smart Contract, always linked to a certain vinate.

Retirement (Burn Function): Action of using the ACS Token to offset emissions using the Token, with due registration with IHS Markit. Transfer: Assignment of the environmental asset to a third party, who may retire or renegotiate the asset.

Vintage: period of generation of a carbon credit.

Compensation Certificate: is a certificate issued jointly by CDSA/SA, Markit and Shiboleth attesting that the buyer has retired his credit, performing an compensation.

Emission Reduction Purchase Agreement - ERPA: is a legal instrument for the assignment of rights related to CDSA/SA Carbon Credits, tokenized in the ACS Token and brokered by Shiboleth.

Greenhouse Gases (GHG): are the gases listed in the Kyoto Protocol of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change. They are: a) carbon dioxide (CO2); b) methane (CH4); c) nitrous oxide (N2O); d) sulfur hexafluoride (SF6); and e) families of hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs) and perfluorocarbons (PFCs) gases.

Registration Entity or Body: it is a body in which the registration of RVEs generated by a compensation project, such as SISA, will be effected. The registrar for ACS Token is IHS Markti (https://ihsmarkit.com). Verified Emission Reductions (RVE): is a measured unit for GHG removal, equivalent to one ton of CO2e, known as a carbon credit.
