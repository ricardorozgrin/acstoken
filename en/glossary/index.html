Important Terms and Concepts

Smart Contract: Technological solution in blockchain on the Ethereum platform, where all ACS Tokens are created, and which records all transactions, transfers and retirements through unique identifier codes.

ACS Token: is the digital unit for a Carbon Credit generated and registered in the context of the SISA project. The amount of the ACS Token is limited to the maximum total of credits generated and registered by CDSA/SA on IHS Markit.

Mint: is the action of adding new ACS Tokens to the Smart Contract, always linked to a certain vinate.

Retirement (Burn Function): Action of using the ACS Token to offset emissions using the Token, with due registration with IHS Markit. Transfer: Assignment of the environmental asset to a third party, who may retire or renegotiate the asset.

Vintage: period of generation of a carbon credit.

Compensation Certificate: is a certificate issued jointly by CDSA/SA, Markit and Shiboleth attesting that the buyer has retired his credit, performing an compensation.

Emission Reduction Purchase Agreement - ERPA: is a legal instrument for the assignment of rights related to CDSA/SA Carbon Credits, tokenized in the ACS Token and brokered by Shiboleth.

Greenhouse Gases (GHG): are the gases listed in the Kyoto Protocol of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change. They are: a) carbon dioxide (CO2); b) methane (CH4); c) nitrous oxide (N2O); d) sulfur hexafluoride (SF6); and e) families of hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs) and perfluorocarbons (PFCs) gases.

Registration Entity or Body: it is a body in which the registration of RVEs generated by a compensation project, such as SISA, will be effected. The registrar for ACS Token is IHS Markti (https://ihsmarkit.com). Verified Emission Reductions (RVE): is a measured unit for GHG removal, equivalent to one ton of CO2e, known as a carbon credit.
